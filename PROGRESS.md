# Experiment Progress

## Completed Files

- `EXP1_kv_importance.md`
  - Main experiment specification.
- `analyze_kv_importance.py`
  - Main KV importance analysis script.
  - Uses a manual decode loop rather than `model.generate()`.
  - Collects `attention[:, :, -1, :]` at each decode step.
  - Aggregates attention over layers and heads by mean.
  - Outputs chunk importance, top-k chunks, stability metrics, attention mass, and plots.
- `plot_pilot_summary.py`
  - Post-processing script for GPT-2 pilot outputs.
  - Creates report-friendly summary plots and subtype-level CSVs.
- `requirements.txt`
  - Python dependencies for running the experiment and plotting.
- `data/samples.jsonl`
  - Long-context debug dataset with 4 samples.
- `data/samples_pilot_40.jsonl`
  - Synthetic GPT-2 pilot dataset with 40 samples:
    - 10 `local`
    - 10 `early_recall`
    - 10 `middle_recall`
    - 10 `comparison`
  - Non-local subtypes use `type: long_range` and preserve the detailed class in `subtype`.
- `.gitignore`
  - Excludes Python bytecode/cache files.

## Current Observation Summary

- The original short debug samples were too short to form a useful Cold KV region.
- The long debug samples fixed this issue: Cold KV mass became nonzero, and each sample had more than two chunks.
- The 40-sample GPT-2 pilot provides a broader synthetic observation set across local, early recall, middle recall, and comparison request patterns.
- In the pilot run, Recent KV received the largest average attention mass, followed by Sink KV and then Cold KV.
- Cold KV attention mass was consistently nonzero in the pilot run.
- The subtype summary suggests `early_recall` had a higher average Cold KV mass than the other synthetic subtypes in this GPT-2 pilot.
- The original `hot_set_overlap.png` was visually crowded because it plotted all 40 sample lines.
- The new summary plots provide cleaner report-oriented views:
  - overall mean hot-set overlap
  - subtype-level hot-set overlap
  - subtype-level attention mass

These are pilot observations only, not final research conclusions.
